## Annexure - III

**[As per Section D of the Model Code of Conduct]**

### A. Definitions

1. **‘Voter’** is defined as any member of the electorate having the voting rights as specified in Section D.2 of the Election Code.
2. **‘Vote’** is defined as the candidate preference of the Voter.
3. **‘Ballot Paper’** refers to the document produced by the Election Commission to cast a vote.
4. **‘Polling Booth’** refers to any publicly accessible on-campus polling location designated by the Election Commission for the use of voting, as defined in Section A.2.
5. **‘Presiding Officer’** refers to the Chief Election Commissioner or a designated Election Commissioner.
6. **‘Invalid Vote’** means a Ballot Paper that cannot be counted due to a violation of vote casting rules.

### B. Design and Printing of Ballot Paper

1. The Election Commission shall design and print all Ballot Papers. Each Ballot Paper shall contain:
   - The name of the election (e.g., General Election, Bye-election, Re-election).
   - The names of all eligible candidates in alphabetical order of their registered IITGN names.
   - A unique computer-recognizable and non-human-readable identifier on the Ballot Paper, recorded in a master log maintained by the Election Commission.
   - An official embossed or stamped seal of the Election Commission, signed by the Chief Election Commissioner. Any Ballot Paper without this seal shall be considered void.
   - A clear instruction on the voting method.
2. The Ballot Paper shall not contain any other content apart from details mentioned in Clause B.1.
3. The Election Commission shall print at least 10% additional blank Ballot Papers marked as "Specimen".

### C. Voter Identification and Issuance of Ballot Paper

1. A Voter must present their valid Election ID Card from the official portal of the Election Commission to the Presiding Officer.
2. Upon verification, the Presiding Officer shall:
   - Mark the Voter’s name off the official electoral roll.
   - Hand over a single, folded Ballot Paper to the Voter.
3. No Voter shall receive more than one Ballot Paper per post.

### D. Voting Procedure

1. After receiving the Ballot Paper, the Voter shall proceed **alone** to a separate, enclosed voting compartment or room within the Polling Booth.
2. No electronic devices or marking equipment are allowed in the compartment or room. If found to have such equipment, the Presiding Officer shall cancel the vote immediately.
3. The Voter shall mark **only one clear vote**, as per the instructions on the Ballot Paper, for their chosen candidate. No other writing, stamp, or marking is permitted.
4. The Voter shall then fold the Ballot Paper **three times horizontally** to conceal their mark and personally deposit it into the Ballot Box.
5. If a Voter makes any error, the Ballot Paper is deemed cancelled. 
6. No Voter shall be allowed to leave the Polling Booth with a Ballot Paper. Any Ballot Paper found or taken outside the Polling Booth shall be considered void.

### E. Invalid Votes (Criteria for Rejection)

A Ballot Paper shall be considered **Invalid** and shall not be counted for any candidate if:

1. It does not bear the official seal of the Election Commission.
2. It contains no mark (blank).
3. It contains marks for more than one candidate.
4. The mark is ambiguous (e.g., multiple marks, ticks and crosses, unclear candidate).
5. The Ballot Paper contains any writing, symbol, or identifying mark (including signatures, comments, or candidate names written outside the designated box) that could potentially identify the Voter.
6. The Ballot Paper is torn, mutilated, or damaged such that the Voter’s intention cannot be clearly determined.
7. The unique identifier on the Ballot Paper does not verify during counting (indicating forgery or tampering).

**Procedure for Invalid Votes:**  
In any of the above cases, the Presiding Officer is required to cancel the vote and display the cancelled Ballot Paper to all candidates of that particular post (or their representatives) present at the Polling Booth. All ambiguous ballots shall be decided by the Presiding Officer only, and the decision shall be recorded in writing.

### F. Counting of Votes

1. Counting shall take place as per the schedule declared by the Election Commission.
2. The maximum delay for the counting of votes shall not exceed **12 hours** from the closure of polling, unless extended by the Election Commission under special circumstances.
3. Ballot Papers shall be taken out, unfolded, and sorted into piles for each candidate, plus a separate pile for Invalid Votes.
4. A minimum of **two Election Commission members** (inclusive of the Presiding Officer) shall observe every count. Candidates or their representatives may visually inspect each Ballot Paper but shall **not touch them**.
5. If a candidate or representative touches a Ballot Paper without due permission from the Election Commission, the Commission shall impose a **Level 3 Penalty** (as per Section E.1), which may lead to the cancellation of candidature.
6. The final count shall be recorded in a **Counting Sheet**, signed by at least two Election Commission members (inclusive of the Presiding Officer).

### G. Violations Specific to Ballot Paper Voting

Any candidate or campaigner found attempting to:

- Tamper with a Ballot Box, seal, or Ballot Paper.
- Photograph a marked Ballot Paper.
- Intimidate a Voter inside the voting compartment.
- Impersonate a Voter.
- Bribe a Voter in exchange for showing a marked Ballot Paper.

shall face a **Level 3 violation** under Section E.1 of the Election Code, with a recommended penalty of **cancellation of candidature** and referral to the Dean of Student Affairs.
